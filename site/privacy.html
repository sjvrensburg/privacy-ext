Privacy

ClipCloak is built so that the text you paste is read on your own computer and nowhere else. This page explains exactly what the extension touches, where the work happens, and what is kept. There is no analytics script behind it, so it can be short.

effective10 July 2026

The short version

ClipCloak does not collect, store on our servers, transmit, sell, or share your data. It has no servers. When you paste into a web form, the pasted text is sent to a daemon running on your own machine (on the loopback address 127.0.0.1), which finds personal information and returns a redacted copy. That exchange stays on your device. We never see it.

What ClipCloak processes

The only content ClipCloak reads is the text of a paste you make into an editable field on a web page. It is read at the moment you paste, so that a redacted version can be offered back to you before anything is committed to the page.

ClipCloak does not read pages you visit, your browsing history, your existing clipboard contents until you paste, form fields you did not paste into, passwords, or cookies. It acts only on the paste event and only on the text within it.

Where the processing happens

Detection runs in a local daemon, the ClipCloak engine, which holds the on-device model. The extension reaches it over HTTP on 127.0.0.1, an address that never leaves your computer and is not exposed to the network. The daemon requires a bearer token, and answers cross origin requests only for the ClipCloak extension, so other local programs and web pages cannot use it.

No remote service is contacted to classify or redact your text. The extension holds no API keys and has no cloud backend. Its host permissions are limited to 127.0.0.1 and localhost for exactly this reason.

What is stored

The pasted text is processed in memory to produce a result and is not written to disk or retained by the daemon after the response is returned. ClipCloak keeps no history of what you have pasted or redacted.

The extension does use your browser's local storage for its own settings: the daemon's port, the detection threshold, and the pairing token used to authenticate to the local daemon. These are configuration values. They stay in your browser profile, are never sent anywhere, and contain none of your pasted content.

Permissions, and why each is needed

Chrome lists the permissions an extension requests. Here is what each one is for in ClipCloak.

content script, all sitescatch the paste in the field you are typing into
host: 127.0.0.1, localhostreach the local daemon, and nothing else
nativeMessagingpair with the desktop app for port and token
storageremember your port, threshold, and token
activeTabinsert your choice back into the current tab

The content script is registered for all sites because a form worth redacting can appear on any site. It does nothing until you paste.

Third parties and disclosure

There are no third parties. ClipCloak does not use analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or any external SDK. Because nothing is collected, there is nothing to sell, rent, or hand over. Your pasted text is never used to train any model.

Changes and contact

If this statement changes, the updated version will be published here with a new effective date, and the change will be visible in the project's public history. Questions, or a report of behaviour that does not match this page, are welcome by email or as an issue on GitHub.
